Day 87 of the Spawning Off-Season

Dear diary,

This morning started as usual: hiding behind the reeds, glaring at the roaches (the fish, not the bugs), and ignoring the carp who always talk too loudly.
Honestly, I don’t know how they’re not eaten faster. Subtlety is an art, and I, being a true European perch, am the Picasso of shadows.

Had a minor territorial dispute with a young pike.
I told him, “You’re not that long yet, buddy.” He didn’t like that. I didn’t care. He flared his fins like he was hot stuff, but I swam a tight circle around him and disappeared into the murk. Classic perch move. Felt pretty good about that one.

- Iso Arska

Day 89 of the Spawning Off-Season

Dear diary,

A group of tourists dropped something shiny from the dock again. Humans are fascinating. Always throwing things into the water, never retrieving them. Today’s gift was a small, silver keychain.

I guarded it for most of the afternoon until a nosy bream tried to nibble it. I chased him halfway across the cove. Victory was mine.
The keychain now rests in my secret collection next to a green plastic ring and a Barbie shoe. One day I will build a shrine. For what purpose, I’m not yet sure. But it will be glorious.

– Iso Arska

Day 93 of the Spawning Off-Season

Dear diary,

I had an existential moment today.
While drifting near the surface, I caught a glimpse of myself reflected in the bottom of a human boat. My stripes looked especially sharp in the sunlight. Do they make me look aggressive? Too aggressive? I've been told I’m “the angry one” of the lake. I prefer “passionately expressive.”

Later, I got stuck in some discarded fishing line. Embarrassing. Thankfully, my dorsal fin is as sharp as my wit. I freed myself in minutes. No one saw… except a smug tench who winked at me. Rude.
Maybe I'll have him for dinner tomorrow.

– Iso Arska

Day 100 of the Spawning Off-Season

Dear diary,

At dusk, the minnows were schooling again. They always move in that hypnotic swirl like they're dancing to music only they can hear. I watched from the shadows, just for the beauty of it. Didn’t even lunge once. Maybe I’m getting soft. Or maybe I’ve just had enough minnow for one week.

Final thoughts for today: The lake is colder now. Winter is stirring beneath the surface. I can feel it in my scales. Fewer bugs, slower snails, and the pike are getting hungrier. I’ll need to be even sharper in the coming days.
But for now, I’m safe, my shrine is growing, and the silver keychain sparkles just right under moonlight.

Until tomorrow,
– Iso Arska
